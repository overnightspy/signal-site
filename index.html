SPY Overnight Gap Forecast

A quantitative model that forecasts the direction of the next SPY overnight gap. Each trading day a single output is published:

  • Up: The Opening price of the next market day will be higher than the closing price of today.
  • Down: The Opening price of the next market day will be lower than the closing price of today.
  • None: Indicates insufficient confidence; an intentional feature reflecting computational epistemic humility; know what you don't know.

Signal generated ~ 6:00 PM ET, outcome verified the next morning.

Validation & QA

Q: Who built this model and why?
A: I'm an American technologist with a finance background. This model addresses one specific question: Will SPY open higher or lower tomorrow? By focusing on a single predictable market event, users can integrate the signal into multiple strategies. Magnitude and monetization are left to the user.

Q: How long will this edge last?
A: The model is designed to be regime-agnostic within its constraints and has forward-tested predictive consistency. Future performance cannot be guaranteed.

Q: Why not share detailed backtests?
A: Backtests are used for development, but they do not reliably indicate forward performance. They are prone to cherry-picking and overfitting.

Q: How can I audit the signal history?
A: All signals are timestamped and posted publicly on Twitter/X, backed by a cryptographically secure ledger for redundant verification.

Q: Is there a secure endpoint for the signal?
A: A secure endpoint is planned for future release to support professional and institutional users.

Q: Why are some signals “None”?
A: “None” indicates the model lacks sufficient confidence. This is a loss-avoidance feature. Additional risk tiers may be offered later.

Q: Why don’t you provide trade strategies?
A: The signal provides directional insight only. There are multiple ways to use it profitably, but developing strategies is outside the scope of this project.
